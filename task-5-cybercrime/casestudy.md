# CASE STUDY: PHISHING ATTACK

Phishing is a type of cybercrime where attackers try to trick people into sharing sensitive information like password, bank details, or OTPs. It usually happens through fake emails, messages, or websites that look real.
A common example is when a student receives an email that looks like it is from their bank or a trusted service. The message may say there is an urgent issue with their account and ask them to click on a link. When the student clicks the link, they are taken to a fake website that looks exactly like the original one. Without realizing, they enter their login details or OTP. The attacker then uses this information to access their account and steal money or data.
Students and young people are often targeted because they use online services frequently but may not always verify links or messages carefully. The consequences of phishing can be serious, including financial loss, data theft, and misuse of personal information.

This shows how important it is to stay alert while using digital platforms and avoid trusting unknown links or messages blindly.
